Issue Spotlight

Key issues affecting District 5 and our community

Housing

Middle Housing Development

Expanding housing options in District 5 through duplexes, triplexes, and small apartment buildings.

Active Issue High Priority

Current Status: Working with city planning to streamline permitting for middle housing projects.

What This Means: More affordable housing options for families, seniors, and young professionals in our district.

  • Simplified zoning for duplexes and triplexes
  • Reduced parking requirements for transit-accessible areas
  • Incentives for affordable units in new developments
  • Community input process for larger projects

Next Steps: Public hearing scheduled for July 15th at City Hall.

Safety

Ember Alert System

Implementing early warning systems for wildfire threats in District 5.

In Progress Critical

Current Status: Installing ember detection sensors and community alert systems.

What This Means: Earlier warnings for residents during wildfire season, giving more time to evacuate safely.

  • Strategic sensor placement in high-risk areas
  • Integration with city emergency systems
  • Community education and evacuation planning
  • Regular system testing and maintenance

Timeline: Phase 1 completion expected by August 2024.

Infrastructure

Marina Fee Structure

Reviewing and updating marina fees to ensure fair access for District 5 residents.

Under Review Medium Priority

Current Status: Conducting community survey and cost analysis.

What This Means: Fair pricing for boat storage, launch fees, and mooring that works for local families.

  • Resident discount program for District 5
  • Sliding scale fees based on boat size
  • Seasonal rate adjustments
  • Maintenance and improvement funding

Community Input: Survey open until July 30th. Your feedback needed!
