Issue Spotlight

Key issues and initiatives affecting Berkeley District 5 residents.

Housing

Middle Housing Development

Learn about opportunities for middle housing development in District 5, including duplexes, triplexes, and small apartment buildings that can help address our housing shortage.

What is Middle Housing?

Middle housing refers to housing types between single-family homes and large apartment buildings. This includes:

  • Duplexes and triplexes
  • Small apartment buildings (4-12 units)
  • Accessory dwelling units (ADUs)
  • Missing middle housing types

Benefits for District 5

  • More affordable housing options
  • Increased housing diversity
  • Better utilization of existing land
  • Support for local businesses

Next Steps: Attend upcoming community meetings to learn about proposed developments and share your input.

Emergency

Ember Alert System

Stay informed about emergency situations in Berkeley through the Ember Alert system, providing real-time notifications about fires, evacuations, and other critical events.

What is Ember Alert?

The Ember Alert system is Berkeley's emergency notification system that provides:

  • Real-time fire and evacuation alerts
  • Emergency weather notifications
  • Public safety announcements
  • Traffic and road closure updates

How to Sign Up

  • Visit the City of Berkeley website
  • Register with your phone number and email
  • Choose your notification preferences
  • Add multiple addresses for monitoring

Important: Make sure your contact information is up to date to receive critical alerts.

Services

Marina Fees and Services

Information about Berkeley Marina fees, services, and upcoming changes. Learn about boat storage, parking, and recreational opportunities at the marina.

Current Marina Services

The Berkeley Marina offers various services including:

  • Boat storage and mooring
  • Recreational facilities
  • Restaurant and retail spaces
  • Event venues and meeting spaces

Fee Structure

  • Daily parking rates
  • Monthly boat storage fees
  • Event venue rental costs
  • Commercial space leases

Upcoming Changes

The city is reviewing marina fee structures to ensure sustainable operations while maintaining accessibility for residents.

Public Input: Share your thoughts on proposed fee changes at upcoming council meetings.

Navigating Local Government FAQs

Common questions about Berkeley city government and services.

How do I report a pothole?

You can report potholes and other street maintenance issues through the City's 311 system or by calling 510-981-2489.

How do I get a building permit?

Building permits are issued by the Planning and Development Department. Visit their office at 2120 Milvia Street or call 510-981-7410.

How do I register to vote?

You can register to vote online at registertovote.ca.gov or pick up a registration form at any public library or post office.
